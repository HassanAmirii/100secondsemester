Module 1: Mathematical Foundations

Estimated Study Time: 8–10 hours

  • 1.1 Numbers and Arithmetic: Natural, integers, rational, irrational, real; fractions, ratios, percentages; powers, roots, standard form; number systems (denary, binary, octal, hexadecimal).
  • 1.2 Set Theory: Definitions (subset, universal, null, unit); operations (union, intersection, complement, symmetric difference); Venn diagrams.
  • 1.3 Algebra and Equations: Algebraic expressions, terms, coefficients; indices and logarithms; factorization, partial fractions; linear & polynomial equations; quadratic equations (roots, discriminant).
  • 1.4 Functions, Graphs, and Inequalities: Domain, range, independent/dependent variables; inverse functions, composition; graphs of linear and second-degree curves; inequalities and absolute values.

Module 2: Core Calculus and Applications

Estimated Study Time: 12–15 hours

  • 2.1 Limits and Continuity: Definition limx→c f(x) = L; evaluating limits (algebraic, trigonometric, at infinity); theorems on limits.
  • 2.2 Differentiation: Geometric interpretation (dy/dx as gradient); standard derivatives (polynomials, trigonometric, log, exponential); product, quotient, chain rule; implicit and parametric differentiation.
  • 2.3 Applications of Differentiation: Tangents and normals; stationary points (maxima, minima, inflection); second and higher derivatives.
  • 2.4 Integration: Area under a curve; indefinite integrals & constant of integration (C); standard integrals, polynomials; integration of functions of a linear function (e.g., ∫ cos(ax+b) dx).

Module 3: Advanced Topics and Specialization

Estimated Study Time: 14–18 hours

  • 3.1 Sequences and Series: Arithmetic (A.P.) and geometric (G.P.) progressions; sum to infinity; power series, Maclaurin’s and Taylor’s series.
  • 3.2 Vectors, Matrices, and Determinants: Matrix definitions, operations, transpose, inverse; determinants (2nd & 3rd order); scalar/vector quantities, dot and cross products.
  • 3.3 Complex Numbers: Symbol j, powers of j; algebra (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division); Argand diagram, polar form.
  • 3.4 Differential Equations: First‑order (direct integration, separating variables, linear); second‑order (homogeneous & inhomogeneous); introduction to Laplace transforms.
  • 3.5 Statistics and Probability: Grouped data, histograms; central tendency (mean, mode, median) and dispersion (standard deviation); basic probability, binomial, Poisson, normal distributions.
