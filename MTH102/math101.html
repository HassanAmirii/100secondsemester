Week 1: Function of a Real Variable

  • Definition of a function; domain and range; mapping real values.
  • Independent and dependent variables.
  • Basic function notation and evaluation.

Week 2: Graphs of Functions

  • Sketching and interpreting graphs of common functions.
  • Intercepts, symmetry, turning points, and asymptotes.
  • Transformations of graphs (shift, stretch, reflection).

Week 3: Limits of Functions

  • Definition of limits and behavior of functions near a point.
  • One-sided limits and limits at infinity.
  • Algebra of limits and standard limit forms.

Week 4: Continuity of Functions

  • Continuity at a point and on an interval.
  • Types of discontinuity.
  • Conditions for continuity of elementary functions.

Week 5: The Derivative as a Limit

  • Derivative from first principles as a limit of incremental ratio.
  • Physical and geometric meaning of derivatives.
  • Tangent and normal concepts.

Week 6: Techniques of Differentiation I

  • Basic differentiation rules for powers and constants.
  • Sum and difference rules.
  • Differentiation of polynomial and simple trigonometric functions.

Week 7: Techniques of Differentiation II

  • Product rule, quotient rule, and chain rule.
  • Implicit differentiation.
  • Differentiation of exponential and logarithmic functions.

Week 8: Integration as the Inverse of Differentiation

  • Antiderivatives and indefinite integrals.
  • Constant of integration.
  • Basic reverse-process connection between differentiation and integration.

Week 9: Techniques of Integration

  • Standard integration formulas.
  • Substitution method.
  • Integration by parts and partial fractions (introductory).

Week 10: Definite Integrals and Applications

  • Evaluation of definite integrals using limits and fundamental rules.
  • Area under curves and between curves.
  • Simple physical and geometric applications of integration.
