Week 1: Origin of Major Ethnic Groups (Northern Nigeria)

  • Hausa: Bayajida Legend, Hausa Bakwai (seven legitimate states), Banza Bakwai (seven illegitimate states).
  • Fulani: nomadic/settled, origins in Futa Jalon, Sokoto Jihad (Usman dan Fodio).
  • Kanuri: Kanem-Bornu Empire, Saifawa Dynasty.
  • Nupe: Edegi/Tsoede, Etsu Nupe.

Week 2: Origin of Major Ethnic Groups (Southern Nigeria)

  • Yoruba: Ife creation myth (Oduduwa), Mecca/Lamurudu legend.
  • Igbo: autochthonous (original inhabitants) vs. migration theories.
  • Benin (Bini): Edo mythology, Obaship.
  • Ijaw (Izon): traditions of origin, migration and settlement.

Week 3: Pre-Colonial Political Systems (Yoruba and Benin)

  • Yoruba (Oyo Empire):
    • Alaafin (king), Oyomesi (council of chiefs), Ogboni cult (religious/political balance).
    • Checks and balances limited the Alaafin’s power.
  • Benin (Bini):
    • Oba (divine king), Uzama (kingmakers), Eghaevbo n’Ogbe (town chiefs).

Week 4: Pre-Colonial Political Systems (Igbo and Ijaw)

  • Igbo:
    • Decentralized / acephalous (no central authority).
    • Governance through age grades, Ozo titled men, village assemblies (direct democracy).
  • Ijaw:
    • Village organization, city‑state system with Amayanabo (king), house system (canoe houses).

Week 5: Arts and Culture

  • Definition: culture = total way of life (material + non‑material).
  • Cultural diversity: Hausa‑Fulani, Yoruba, Igbo, Ijaw, Kanuri, Tiv, Efik, Nupe.
  • Arts & crafts: bronze (Igbo‑Ukwu, Ife, Benin), terra‑cotta, wood carving, textiles (Aso‑oke, Akwete).
  • Languages: Niger‑Congo (majority), Nilo‑Saharan (Kanuri), Afro‑Asiatic (Hausa).
  • Dressing: Aso‑oke (Yoruba), Isi‑agu (Igbo), tribal marks, body adornment.

Week 6: Trade and Self-Reliance

  • Trade: international, domestic, barter, grey market.
  • Economic self-reliance: autarky (national self-sufficiency), import substitution industrialization (ISI).

Week 7: Social Justice and National Development

  • Social justice: equality, equity, human rights, inclusion.
  • Theories: Liberalism, Marxism, Feminism, Critical Theory, Capabilities Approach (Sen/Nussbaum), Postcolonialism.
  • Link to national development: justice fosters stability and growth.
  • Historical injustice: colonial legacies, social movements.
  • Dimensions: economic, political, social, human rights.

Week 9: Re-orientation, Moral and National Values

  • Moral values: integrity, honesty, respect, responsibility.
  • National values: identity, unity, democratic governance, patriotism.
  • Moral decay factors: social, cultural, economic, technology/media.
  • Philosophical foundations: Utilitarianism (greatest good), Deontology (duty), Virtue Ethics (character).
  • Ethical leadership: accountability, transparency; corruption as an obstacle.
