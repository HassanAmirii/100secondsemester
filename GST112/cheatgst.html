GST112: Complete Cheat Sheet

Nigerian Peoples and Culture – origins · politics · arts · ethics

1. Origins of Major Ethnic Groups

  • Northern Nigeria (Week 1) Hausa: Bayajida legend; Hausa Bakwai (seven legitimate states) and Banza Bakwai (illegitimate).
    Fulani: Futa Jalon origin; nomadic/pastoral; Sokoto Jihad (Usman dan Fodio).
    Kanuri: Kanem‑Bornu Empire, Saifawa Dynasty.
    Nupe: Edegi/Tsoede, Etsu Nupe.
  • Southern Nigeria (Week 2) Yoruba: Ife creation (Oduduwa); Mecca/Lamurudu legend.
    Igbo: Autochthonous (original inhabitants) vs. migration theories.
    Benin (Bini): Edo mythology; Obaship.
    Ijaw (Izon): Traditions of origin, migration and settlement.

2. Pre‑Colonial Political Systems

  • Yoruba (Oyo Empire) Alaafin (king), Oyomesi (council), Ogboni (religious balance) – checks and balances limited the Alaafin’s power.
  • Benin (Bini) Oba (divine king), Uzama (kingmakers), Eghaevbo n’Ogbe (town chiefs).
  • Igbo Decentralized/acephalous; governance through age grades, Ozo titled men, village assemblies (direct democracy).
  • Ijaw Village organisation, city‑states with Amayanabo (king), house system (canoe houses).

3. Culture, Arts & Economic Concepts

  • Arts & Culture (Week 5) Culture: total way of life (material + non‑material).
    Diversity: Hausa‑Fulani, Yoruba, Igbo, Ijaw, Kanuri, Tiv, Efik, Nupe.
    Arts & crafts: bronze (Igbo‑Ukwu, Ife, Benin), terra‑cotta, wood carving, textiles (Aso‑oke, Akwete).
    Languages: Niger‑Congo (majority), Nilo‑Saharan (Kanuri), Afro‑Asiatic (Hausa).
    Dressing: Aso‑oke (Yoruba), Isi‑agu (Igbo), tribal marks.
  • Trade & Self‑Reliance (Week 6) Trade types: international, domestic, barter, grey market.
    Self‑reliance: autarky (national self‑sufficiency), import substitution industrialization (ISI).

4. Ethics, Values & Development

  • Social Justice (Week 7) Core ideas: equality, equity, human rights, inclusion.
    Theories: Liberalism, Marxism, Feminism, Critical Theory, Capabilities Approach, Postcolonialism.
    Link to development: justice fosters stability and growth.
    Dimensions of injustice: economic, political, social, human rights. Colonial legacies, social movements.
  • Re‑orientation & National Values (Week 9) Moral values: integrity, honesty, respect, responsibility.
    National values: identity, unity, democratic governance, patriotism.
    Moral decay factors: social, cultural, economic, technology/media.
    Philosophical foundations: Utilitarianism (greatest good), Deontology (duty), Virtue Ethics (character).
    Ethical leadership: accountability, transparency; corruption as an obstacle.
