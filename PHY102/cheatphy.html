PHY102: Complete Cheat Sheet

electric charge · DC circuits · electromagnetic induction

Week 1: Electric Charge and Field

  • Coulomb's Law F = k·q₁q₂ / r² where k = 8.99×10⁹ N·m²/C²
    Force is attractive for opposite charges, repulsive for like charges.
  • Electric Field E = F / q (N/C). For a point charge: E = k·q / r² (direction away from +q).
  • Electric Potential Difference (Voltage) V = W / q (work per unit charge). For a point charge: V = k·q / r.

Weeks 2–3: DC Principles

  • Electric Current I = ΔQ / Δt (amperes).
  • Ohm's Law V = I R
  • Resistance & Resistivity R = ρ L / A (ρ = resistivity, L = length, A = cross‑sectional area).
  • Temperature Dependence R(T) = R₀ [1 + α (T – T₀)] (α = temperature coefficient).
  • Electric Power P = I V = I² R = V² / R (watts).
  • Electrical Energy E = P · t (joules or kWh).

Week 4: Resistor Networks

  • Series Resistance Rₜ = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + … (same current through all).
  • Parallel Resistance 1/Rₜ = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + … (same voltage across all).
  • Special case (two resistors in parallel) Rₜ = (R₁ R₂) / (R₁ + R₂)

Week 5: EMF, Internal Resistance, Kirchhoff

  • Terminal Voltage V = EMF – I r (r = internal resistance).
  • Kirchhoff's Junction Rule Sum of currents entering a junction = sum of currents leaving.
  • Kirchhoff's Loop Rule Sum of potential differences around any closed loop = 0.

Week 6: Electromagnetic Induction

  • Magnetic Flux Φ = B·A·cosθ (webers).
  • Faraday's Law ε = –N ΔΦ / Δt (induced EMF).
  • Lenz's Law The induced current opposes the change in flux (explains the minus sign).
  • Transformer Equation Vₛ / Vₚ = Nₛ / Nₚ (voltage ratio = turns ratio).
  • Self‑Inductance ε = –L dI/dt (L = inductance in henrys).

Week 7: Key Problem‑Solving Tips

  • Coulomb / Field Use vector addition for multiple charges; remember field direction.
  • DC Circuits Combine resistors step‑by‑step; apply Ohm’s law and Kirchhoff for multi‑loop.
  • Induction Sign of EMF from Lenz’s law; flux change can be due to changing B, area, or angle.
