WK 1: Foundational Concepts of Electric Charge and Field

  • Electric Charge: protons (+), electrons (−); conservation of charge.
  • Coulomb's Law: electric force F = k·q₁q₂/r² (k = 8.99×10⁹ N·m²/C²).
  • Electric Field: E = F/q; field lines originate on +, terminate on −.
  • Electric Potential Difference (Voltage): V = W/q (energy per unit charge).

WK 2: Basic Principles of Direct Current (DC) I

  • Electric Current: I = ΔQ/Δt (amperes).
  • Conductors (allow free electron flow) vs. Insulators (resist flow).
  • Ohm's Law: V = I R (voltage = current × resistance).

WK 3: Basic Principles of Direct Current (DC) II

  • Resistance & Resistivity: R = ρ L/A (ρ = resistivity, L = length, A = cross‑section).
  • Temperature effect: R(T) = R₀[1 + α(T - T₀)] (α = temperature coefficient).
  • Electric Power:
    • P = I V
    • P = I² R
    • P = V² / R
  • Energy: E = P · t (joules or kilowatt‑hours).

WK 4: DC Circuit Analysis I

  • Resistors in Series: total resistance Rₜ = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + … (same current).
  • Resistors in Parallel: equivalent resistance 1/Rₜ = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + … (same voltage).

WK 5: DC Circuit Analysis II

  • Electromotive Force (EMF): voltage provided by a source (battery).
  • Internal Resistance (r): terminal voltage V = EMF − I r.
  • Kirchhoff's Rules:
    • Junction rule: sum of currents entering = sum leaving (charge conservation).
    • Loop rule: sum of potential differences around any closed loop = 0 (energy conservation).

WK 6: Electromagnetic Induction

  • Link between electricity and magnetism: moving charges create magnetic fields; changing fields induce EMF.
  • Faraday's Law: induced EMF ε = −N ΔΦ/Δt (Φ = magnetic flux).
  • Lenz's Law: induced current opposes the change in flux (negative sign).
  • Transformers: change AC voltage using mutual induction; Vₛ/Vₚ = Nₛ/Nₚ.
  • Self / Mutual Induction: inductance L; induced EMF ε = −L dI/dt.
