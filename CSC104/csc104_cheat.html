CSC104: Data Analysis Cheat Sheet

big data · data quality · modeling · warehousing · business impact

1. Introduction to Data Analysis & Big Data

  • Data Science Lifecycle Capture → Maintain → Process → Analyze → Communicate
  • Data Types Structured: tabular, predefined schema (e.g., SQL tables).
    Unstructured: text, images, video (no schema).
    Semi‑structured: XML, JSON – some organization but not fully structured.
  • Data Quality Dimensions Accuracy, Consistency, Timeliness, Completeness, Relevance, Validity.
  • 4 Vs of Big Data Volume (scale), Velocity (speed of generation), Variety (different forms), Veracity (uncertainty/quality).

2. Data Modeling and Management Tools

  • Metadata “Data about data”. Characteristics: descriptive, structured, contextual, relational, dynamic. Merits: better understanding, discovery. Demerits: overhead, maintenance.
  • Data Flow Diagrams (DFD) Graphical representation of data movement. Components: processes, data flows, data stores, external entities. Levels: context (0‑level), 1‑level, 2‑level (decomposition). Logical vs. physical DFDs.
  • Other Diagrams ERD (entity‑relationship), UML class diagrams, network diagrams.
  • Databases Operational databases: day‑to‑day transactions.
    Relational (SQL): tables with relationships.
    Non‑relational (NoSQL): document, key‑value, graph, columnar.

3. Advanced Data Storage & Analytics

  • Data Warehouse (DW) Subject‑oriented, integrated, non‑volatile, time‑variant. Architecture: sources → ETL → staging → data storage (ODS, data marts) → access tools (query, reporting, OLAP). Benefits: unified view, historical analysis. Challenges: scalability, data quality, integration cost.
  • Data Management Platform (DMP) Used in marketing/advertising. Collects first‑party, second‑party, third‑party data; integrates, normalizes, segments audiences.
  • Statistical Modeling Descriptive: summarise data (mean, median).
    Inferential: draw conclusions from samples.
    Forecasting: predictive modeling, time series analysis.

4. Business Applications of Data

  • Key Impact Areas
    • Informed decision making & business analytics
    • Operational efficiency & optimisation
    • Customer understanding & personalisation
    • Risk management & fraud detection
    • Market intelligence & competitive advantage
    • Product development & innovation
    • Compliance & regulatory requirements
